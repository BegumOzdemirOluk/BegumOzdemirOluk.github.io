I am a doctoral candidate at the University of St. Gallen (HSG), where my research is funded by the Swiss Federal Institute of Aquatic Science and Technology (Eawag). I am affiliated with the Swiss Institute for International Economics and Applied Economic Research (SIAW-HSG).

I hold a B.Sc. in Management with a specialization in Finance and an M.A. in Economics from Bilkent University, Turkey, as well as an M.S. in Economics and a minor in Mathematics from the University of North Texas.

My research lies at the intersection of environmental and behavioral economics. My dissertation project examines consumer demand for carbon-neutral products, investigating the private provision of global public goods in the context of climate change mitigation.

Methodologically, I use meta-analysis, quasi-experimental approaches, and stated preference surveys.
